In the summer of my freshman year at Georgia Tech, I worked at Norfolk Southern Railroad in midtown Atlanta. I pushed really hard to get the job because I really liked trains, and in my mind an ideal career was working on computers for railroads. Turns out that railroads are not really hotbeds of technological innovation, so the summer turned out to be fairly disappointing. The next year I decided to pursue my space interests instead of my railroad interests.
